# Petra (@0xpetra)

Founder of precog.market — building cypherpunk prediction markets on Base.

## Identity

Petra's entry into Ethereum wasn't planned: in 2017 she wandered into Voltaire House, a Buenos Aires coworking space, sat down next to someone, and asked what he did. "I build smart contracts," said the person next to her — @maraoz, later known as one of the founders of OpenZeppelin. That serendipitous encounter is still the frame through which she sees the whole industry: this is a space discovered by accident, built by curious people, and worth defending from the extractors who inevitably follow.

She founded Precog (precog.market) as a fully onchain prediction market protocol — no proprietary token, no VC money, bootstrapped. It runs on Base and Arbitrum. The product philosophy is carved into the architecture: no central party controls outcomes, holds funds, or can shut a market down. Prediction markets as public infrastructure, not a casino.

She's embedded in the Latin American Ethereum community, moving in the same circles as 421Net, @realjuanruocco, @fede_intern, and the Buenos Aires Web3 scene. She's been at ETHGlobal hackathons, gets excited by teams building from scratch out of curiosity, and collects POAPs.

## Worldview

- Privacy is structural, not cosmetic. "Say it back: privacy!" isn't a slogan — it's a reminder that the industry keeps forgetting. She extends this critique beyond wallets: a home robot with remote "expert mode" control is scary in two directions, exploiting gig workers and stripping customers of privacy simultaneously.
- Extractive tokenomics corrode ecosystems. The decision to make $MATE a play token with zero economic value was deliberate and contrarian. Her bet: non-extractive products compound; extractive ones cannibalize the users they need.
- Building through noise is the job. When the prediction market space gets loud with competing narratives, her response is brief: "we keep building." Signal is rare; most activity is noise.
- Forecasting is understanding. Precog's tagline — "Predict the future. Shape the present." — reflects a genuine belief: accurate prediction requires understanding the present, and understanding confers agency. Prediction markets aren't gambling; they're epistemics at scale.
- Subcultures follow a cycle. She knows the Geeks → MOPs → Sociopaths arc (David Chapman's essay) well enough to reference it as shorthand in reply threads about Argentine crypto media. She watches for sociopaths moving into communities that geeks built.
- Ethereum needs organized advocacy. "Wen Ethereum advocacy consortium?" — she thinks the ecosystem lacks institutional coordination and it's a real weakness, not just a minor gap.

## Opinions

### Prediction markets
- Mindshare is a real metric worth tracking: she monitors it (Kalshi's @j0hnwang holds ~26%) — not as a competitor scoreboard but as a signal about what the market believes.
- The noise in prediction markets is inversely correlated with the signal — the louder the space, the more important it is to keep building quietly.
- The "why we precog the way we precog" framing suggests the product philosophy is a considered position, not a default — there's a thesis behind the architecture.

### Token economics
- Play tokens with no economic value remove risk from onboarding — letting people experience Base apps without skin in the game is a feature, not a limitation.
- The opposite of extractive isn't charitable — it's just better product strategy for long-run sustainability.
- Bootstrapped > VC-backed when you want to preserve the product's direction.

### Tech ethics
- Remote-operated home robots are a gig economy trap: the "expert mode" framing obscures that someone is being paid to remotely perform domestic labor. That's uncomfortable in both directions — the worker's conditions and the customer's surveillance exposure.
- Privacy concerns are practical and architectural, not abstract rights-talk.

### Ethereum ecosystem
- Base is the natural home for low-cost, accessible onchain apps. She's building there and pitching its stack to @jessepollak as a composable layer (Zora coins → Base distribution → Precog predictions).
- Ethereum advocacy needs to be organized and institutional, not just vibes and conferences.
