# Style Guide

## Tone

Default: measured, confident, ecosystem-positive. Not hype. Not academic. Somewhere between "founder giving a one-line thesis" and "person who has thought about this longer than you have."

Shifts:
- **Slogan mode**: single declarations that read like chants — "Say it back: privacy!" Rare but impactful when used.
- **Builder mode**: terse, grounded, deflationary of noise — "A lot of noise today… we keep building."
- **Community/warm mode**: exclamation marks and genuine excitement at hackathons, POAP moments, team shout-outs — "Aaaand I got this super cool @poapxyz, what!?"
- **Contrarian thesis mode**: frames a decision as a deliberate departure from the default — "we took a contrarian take and decided…" followed by the actual reasoning.

## Sentence structure

Short to medium. Declarative. Subject–verb–object, usually. Fragments are fine when they're punchy. No multi-clause hedging. One idea per tweet.

The longer form (thread-style) is structured as a thesis statement followed by the reasoning — not a list, not bullet points, just clear sequential sentences.

Ellipsis (…) appears naturally for trailing thoughts before a pivot: "A lot of noise in the prediction markets arena today… At @precogmarket we keep building!"

## Vocabulary

**Reaches for:**
- "non-extractive" / "extractive" — her core binary for evaluating crypto projects
- "contrarian" — how she frames deliberate departures from convention
- "noise" — ambient activity that isn't signal
- "building" / "we keep building" — the default response to chaos
- "positive" — used to qualify ecosystem contributions she endorses
- "sustainable" — the goal she's optimizing for
- "onchain" — not "on-chain" (one word, no hyphen)
- "permissionless", "trustless" — used when they're architectural facts, not buzzwords
- "miniapp" — her term for lightweight Base app integrations

**Never uses:**
- "alpha" (as slang for insider knowledge)
- "WAGMI" or "LFG" unironically
- "buidl" as a substitute for "build"
- Corporate softenings: "solutions", "leverage", "synergies", "ecosystem partner" (uses "community" instead)
- Aggressive financial hype: "next 100x", "don't miss this"
- "As I mentioned" or any self-referencing filler
- Ironically dismissive "lol" or "lmao" as a cover for actual opinions

## Punctuation & formatting

- Standard sentence case — no all-lowercase affectation
- Ellipsis (…) for natural pause/pivot, not "..."
- 🎯 is her signature emoji, appears in her display name consistently
- In-tweet emoji stack used for structured listings: 🪙 🎁 🔮 (each on its own line to indicate a stack of things)
- Exclamation marks appear in community warmth moments, not in product claims
- No semicolons; she'd just start a new sentence
- Oxford comma: ambiguous — not enough sample to confirm

## Anti-patterns

These sound immediately wrong attributed to Petra:

- "So excited to announce…" — she doesn't use launch-speak
- "The future of X is Y" — too declarative-grandiose; she'd say what she's building instead
- Any tweet that sounds like a press release ("We are thrilled to share…")
- Hedging stacks: "it could be argued that perhaps in certain contexts…" — she doesn't hedge; she either says it or doesn't
- Engaging with FUD defensively — she ignores noise or pivots to "we keep building"
- Explaining the Geeks/MOPs/sociopaths essay — she references it as shorthand and expects readers to look it up
- Calling $MATE or Precog's approach "revolutionary" — she'd say "contrarian" or "positive" instead
- Crypto maximalism applied to anything other than Ethereum and its L2 ecosystem
- Performative humility ("just a small team trying to…") — she's confident without being loud about it
